Cultivation Guide

Master mango farming with these detailed steps:

1. Site Selection and Preparation

Climate: Mangoes thrive in tropical and subtropical climates with distinct dry and wet seasons. Ideal temperature range is 24-30°C.

Soil: Well-drained, deep loamy soil with pH 5.5-7.5 is optimal. Avoid waterlogged areas.

Land Preparation: Clear the land of weeds and debris. Dig pits of 1m × 1m × 1m size at a spacing of 10m × 10m for traditional varieties and 5m × 5m for high-density planting.

Pit Filling: Fill pits with a mixture of topsoil, farmyard manure (20-25 kg), and 2 kg single super phosphate. Allow to settle for 2-3 weeks before planting.

2. Planting and Varieties

Planting Season: June-July or February-March are ideal planting times in Maharashtra.

Planting Method: Place the sapling in the center of the pit, ensuring the graft joint is above ground level. Firm the soil around the plant and water immediately.

Recommended Varieties for Maharashtra:

  • Alphonso (Hapus): Premium variety with excellent flavor and aroma.
  • Kesar: Known for its saffron-colored pulp and sweet taste.
  • Ratna: Early-maturing hybrid with good yield.
  • Pairi: Good for table and processing purposes.
  • Totapuri: Suitable for processing, high-yielding.

3. Irrigation and Nutrition

Irrigation Schedule:

  • Young trees (1-3 years): Water every 7-10 days.
  • Bearing trees: Critical irrigation periods are flowering, fruit set, and fruit development.
  • Water stress during 2-3 months before flowering induces better flowering.

Fertilizer Application:

  • 1st year: 500g N, 250g P, 250g K per tree.
  • 2nd year: 1000g N, 500g P, 500g K per tree.
  • 3rd year onwards: Increase by 500g N, 250g P, 250g K annually until 10th year.

Apply in two splits: June-July and October-November.

Micronutrients: Foliar spray of zinc sulfate (0.5%) and manganese sulfate (0.5%) during new flush emergence helps correct deficiencies.

4. Pest and Disease Management

Common Pests:

  • Mango Hopper: Spray imidacloprid (0.3 ml/L) or thiamethoxam (0.2 g/L) at flowering stage.
  • Fruit Fly: Install pheromone traps (5/ha) and bag fruits at marble stage.
  • Mealy Bug: Apply chlorpyriphos (2 ml/L) on tree trunk.

Major Diseases:

  • Powdery Mildew: Spray wettable sulfur (2g/L) or dinocap (1 ml/L).
  • Anthracnose: Apply carbendazim (1g/L) or copper oxychloride (3g/L).
  • Die Back: Prune affected parts and apply copper oxychloride paste on cut ends.

Integrated Pest Management: Maintain orchard sanitation, use biological controls when possible, and apply chemicals only when necessary.

5. Harvesting and Post-Harvest Handling

Harvesting: Harvest when fruits are mature but still firm. For Alphonso, look for slight yellow coloration at the stem end.

Harvesting Method: Use a mango harvester with a net bag to prevent fruit damage. Cut with 4-5 cm stalk attached.

Grading: Sort fruits based on size, color, and quality.

Packaging: Pack in single layer in corrugated fiber boxes with proper ventilation.

Storage: Store at 12-13°C with 85-90% relative humidity for up to 3 weeks.

Ripening: For commercial ripening, use ethylene (100 ppm) in ripening chambers at 20-22°C.

6. Organic Mango Cultivation

Soil Management: Apply compost, vermicompost, and green manures to improve soil health.

Nutrient Management: Use organic inputs like farmyard manure (50 kg/tree), neem cake (5 kg/tree), and vermicompost (20 kg/tree).

Pest Management:

  • Neem oil (2%) for controlling hoppers and mealy bugs.
  • Trichoderma viride for controlling soil-borne diseases.
  • Beauveria bassiana for fruit fly control.

Certification: Contact accredited certification agencies for organic certification, which typically requires a 3-year conversion period.

Market Premium: Organic mangoes can fetch 20-30% higher prices in domestic and export markets.

About Us

The Maharashtra Mango Cultivators Platform is dedicated to empowering mango farmers across Maharashtra with cutting-edge technology and AI-driven solutions. Our mission is to increase productivity, reduce crop losses, and improve the livelihoods of mango farmers through accessible digital tools.

By combining artificial intelligence, weather forecasting, and agricultural expertise, we aim to revolutionize mango farming practices and create a sustainable future for mango cultivation in the region.

Our Technology

At the heart of our platform is a sophisticated AI model trained to identify various mango leaf diseases with high accuracy. Using MobileNetV2 architecture, our system can detect 8 different conditions including Anthracnose, Bacterial Canker, Cutting Weevil, Die Back, Gall Midge, Powdery Mildew, and more.

Our weather forecasting system provides localized predictions specifically tailored for mango orchards, helping farmers make informed decisions about irrigation, pest control, and harvest timing.
